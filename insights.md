# F1 Driver Analytics - Key Insights

## Project Objective
This project analyzes historical Formula 1 driver performance using SQL, Excel, and Power BI to identify patterns in driver dominance, consistency, efficiency, and championship success.

---

# Basic Analysis Insights

## 1. Driver Dominance
- Lewis Hamilton and Michael Schumacher emerged among the most dominant drivers in Formula 1 history based on total race wins.
- Drivers with high podium counts demonstrated exceptional long-term consistency across multiple seasons.

## 2. Nationality Analysis
- British and German drivers contributed the highest number of total race wins historically.
- Certain countries consistently produced elite Formula 1 talent across multiple decades.

## 3. Performance Efficiency
- Modern-era drivers achieved significantly higher points-per-entry ratios due to evolving points systems and improved car performance.
- Some drivers maintained high efficiency despite participating in fewer races.

## 4. Championship Trends
- Championship victories were heavily concentrated among a small group of elite drivers.
- Specific decades showed stronger dominance by individual drivers compared to others.

---

# Intermediate Analysis Insights

## 5. Champion vs Non-Champion Comparison
- Champion drivers significantly outperformed non-champions across all major performance indicators.
- Champions maintained substantially higher:
  - Win rates
  - Podium consistency
  - Pole position efficiency
  - Points per race entry

## 6. Hidden Elite Drivers
- Several drivers achieved impressive win rates despite never winning a world championship.
- This suggests that external factors such as team competitiveness and reliability also influence championship outcomes.

## 7. Efficiency Analysis
- Some legendary drivers secured championships with remarkably fewer race entries, indicating exceptional career efficiency.
- Drivers with lower total wins occasionally displayed superior consistency metrics.

## 8. Decade-Based Evolution
- Formula 1 performance metrics evolved considerably across decades.
- Modern eras show increased scoring opportunities and stronger points accumulation trends.

---

# Advanced Analysis Insights

## 9. GOAT Score Analysis
- A custom GOAT scoring model was developed using:
  - Win Rate
  - Podium Rate
  - Pole Rate
  - Points Per Entry

- The scoring model revealed that podium consistency contributes significantly to long-term driver dominance.

## 10. Driver Tier Classification
Drivers were segmented into:
- Elite Drivers
- Great Drivers
- Competitive Drivers
- Developing Drivers

This classification helped identify performance clusters within Formula 1 history.

## 11. Era Dominance Analysis
- Window functions and ranking analysis revealed the most dominant drivers within each Formula 1 decade.
- Certain eras were dominated by a small number of elite performers.

---

# Overall Conclusion

The analysis demonstrates that Formula 1 success is influenced not only by total race victories but also by long-term consistency, efficiency, and adaptability across competitive eras.

The project highlights how advanced SQL analytics and data visualization techniques can uncover deeper performance insights in sports analytics.
